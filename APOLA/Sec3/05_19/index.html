05_19

題目​

If A\bm{A}A is a skew-Hermitian, show that both (I−A)(\bm{I}-\bm{A})(I−A) and (I+A)(\bm{I}+\bm{A})(I+A) are invertible and that C=(I−A)(I+A)−1\bm{C} = (\bm{I}-\bm{A})(\bm{I}+\bm{A})^{-1}C=(I−A)(I+A)−1 is unitary.

解答​

By Sec 3.5 Q16 we have all eigenvalue of A\bm{A}A are pure imaginary, then all eigenvalues of I±A\bm{I\pm A}I±A are not zero, then both of them are invertible.

Since

C∗=((I+A)−1)∗(I−A∗)=((I+A)∗)−1(I−A∗)=(I+A∗)−1(I+A)=(I−A)−1(I+A)\begin{align*} \bm{C}^* &= \big({(\bm{I+A})}^{-1}\big)^*(\bm{I-A}^*)\\ &= \big({(\bm{I+A})}^*\big)^{-1}(\bm{I-A}^*)\\ &= {(\bm{I}+\bm{A}^*)}^{-1} (\bm{I+A})\\ &= (\bm{I-A})^{-1}(\bm{I+A}) \end{align*}C∗​=((I+A)−1)∗(I−A∗)=((I+A)∗)−1(I−A∗)=(I+A∗)−1(I+A)=(I−A)−1(I+A)​

and (I−A)(I+A)=I−A2=(I+A)(I−A)(\bm{I-A})(\bm{I+A}) = \bm{I}-\bm{A}^2 = (\bm{I+A})(\bm{I-A})(I−A)(I+A)=I−A2=(I+A)(I−A).

Hence

C∗C=(I−A)−1(I+A)(I−A)(I+A)−1=(I−A)−1(I−A)(I+A)(I+A)−1=I\begin{align*} \bm{C}^*\bm{C} &= (\bm{I-A})^{-1}(\bm{I+A})(\bm{I-A})(\bm{I+A})^{-1}\\ &= (\bm{I-A})^{-1}(\bm{I-A})(\bm{I+A})(\bm{I+A})^{-1}\\ & = \bm{I} \end{align*}C∗C​=(I−A)−1(I+A)(I−A)(I+A)−1=(I−A)−1(I−A)(I+A)(I+A)−1=I​
