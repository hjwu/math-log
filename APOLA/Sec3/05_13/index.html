題目​

If A∈Mn(R)\bm{A} \in \mathcal{M}_n(\mathbb{R})A∈Mn​(R) is such that (Av,Av)=(v,v)(\bm{Av},\bm{Av}) = (\bm{v},\bm{v})(Av,Av)=(v,v) for all v∈Rn\bm{v} \in \mathbb{R}^nv∈Rn, is A∗A\bm{A}^*\bm{A}A∗A necessary equal to I\bm{I}I ? Either prove or produce a counterexample.

解答​

Yes. Since

(v,A∗Av)=(Av,Av)=(v,v)=(v,Iv),(\bm{v},\bm{A}^*\bm{Av})=(\bm{Av},\bm{Av}) = (\bm{v},\bm{v}) = (\bm{v},\bm{Iv}),(v,A∗Av)=(Av,Av)=(v,v)=(v,Iv),

then 0=(v,(A∗A−I)v)0 = \big(\bm{v},(\bm{A}^*\bm{A}-\bm{I})\bm{v}\big)0=(v,(A∗A−I)v) for all v\bm{v}v.

Thus we have A∗A−I=O\bm{A}^*\bm{A}-\bm{I} = \bm{O}A∗A−I=O.
