題目​

If TTT is unitary linear transformation of Fn\mathcal{F}^nFn, and T(v1),⋯ ,T(vn)T(v_1), \cdots, T(v_n)T(v1​),⋯,T(vn​) is an orthonomoral basis of Fn\mathcal{F}^nFn, show that v1,⋯ ,vnv_1, \cdots, v_nv1​,⋯,vn​ is an orthonomoral basis of Fn\mathcal{F}^nFn.

解答​

For all i≠ji\neq ji=j we have

⟨vi,vj⟩=⟨vi,I(vj)⟩=⟨vi,T∗T(vj)⟩=⟨T(vi),T(vj)⟩=0\langle v_i, v_j \rangle = \langle v_i, \bm{I}(v_j)\rangle = \langle v_i, T^{*}T(v_j)\rangle = \langle T(v_i), T(v_j)\rangle = 0⟨vi​,vj​⟩=⟨vi​,I(vj​)⟩=⟨vi​,T∗T(vj​)⟩=⟨T(vi​),T(vj​)⟩=0

then it's orthonomoral.

If a1v1+a2v2+⋯anvn=0a_{1}v_{1} + a_{2}v_{2} + \cdots a_{n}v_{n} = 0a1​v1​+a2​v2​+⋯an​vn​=0, then

0=T(a1v1+a2v2+⋯+anvn)=a1T(v1)+a2T(v2)+⋯+anT(vn).\begin{align*} 0=&T(a_{1}v_{1} + a_{2}v_{2} + \cdots + a_{n}v_{n})\\ =&a_{1}T(v_{1}) + a_{2}T(v_{2}) + \cdots + a_{n}T(v_{n}). \end{align*}0==​T(a1​v1​+a2​v2​+⋯+an​vn​)a1​T(v1​)+a2​T(v2​)+⋯+an​T(vn​).​

Since T(v1),⋯ ,T(vn)T(v_1), \cdots, T(v_n)T(v1​),⋯,T(vn​) is an orthonomoral basis then ai=0a_i = 0ai​=0 for all iii, then it's basis.
